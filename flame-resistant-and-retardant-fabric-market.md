<p><p>The flame-resistant and retardant fabric market is categorized into two main types: inherent and treated. Inherent flame-resistant fabrics are made from fibers that naturally resist flames, offering durable protection without chemical treatments. These materials retain their protective qualities throughout their lifespan. In contrast, treated flame-resistant fabrics are those that undergo chemical processes to enhance their fire resistance. While effective, these treatments may diminish over time, necessitating reapplications to maintain their protective properties. Both types serve critical roles in various industries.</p></p>
