<p><p>The freshly ground coffee market is segmented into three main types based on grind size: rough grinding, medium grinding, and fine grinding. Rough grinding produces coarser particles, ideal for methods like French press and cold brew. Medium grinding offers a balance, suitable for drip coffee makers and pour-over methods. Fine grinding, with its very small particles, is perfect for espresso and Turkish coffee. Each type caters to different brewing techniques, influencing extraction rates and flavor profiles, thus appealing to diverse consumer preferences.</p></p>
