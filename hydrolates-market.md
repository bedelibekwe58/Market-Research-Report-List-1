<p><p>Hydrolates, also known as flower waters or hydrosols, are aqueous byproducts obtained from the steam distillation of plant materials, primarily flowers, leaves, and herbs. They retain the aromatic and therapeutic properties of the plants, making them popular in cosmetics, aromatherapy, and natural medicine. The demand for hydrolates has been rising due to the increasing consumer inclination towards natural and organic products.</p><p>The hydrolates market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.8% during the forecast period, driven by the growing awareness of the benefits of natural ingredients in personal care and wellness products. Notable trends include the rise of clean beauty, where consumers prioritize products free from synthetic chemicals, prompting manufacturers to incorporate hydrolates for their soothing and hydrating properties. Additionally, the surge in e-commerce platforms has facilitated wider access to diverse hydrolate products, further boosting market growth.</p><p>Innovations in product formulations and the introduction of new hydrolates from exotic plants are also gaining traction.
