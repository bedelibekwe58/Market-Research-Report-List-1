<p><p>The food grade glucose market is primarily divided into two types: monohydrate glucose and anhydrous glucose. Monohydrate glucose contains one molecule of water per molecule of glucose, providing stability and moisture retention, making it ideal for baking and confectionery. Anhydrous glucose, devoid of water, offers a more concentrated form and is preferred in applications requiring higher sweetness intensity or specific consistency. Both types serve various industries, including food and beverages, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics, based on their distinct chemical properties.</p></p>
