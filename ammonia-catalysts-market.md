<p><p>The ammonia catalysts market is primarily segmented into iron-based and ruthenium-based catalysts. Iron-based catalysts are widely used due to their cost-effectiveness and efficiency in high-temperature conditions, making them suitable for large-scale ammonia synthesis processes. In contrast, ruthenium-based catalysts offer superior activity and selectivity, operating effectively at lower temperatures. While iron catalysts dominate the market due to their affordability, ruthenium catalysts are gaining traction for specialized applications where higher performance is required, reflecting diverse technology needs in ammonia production.</p></p>
