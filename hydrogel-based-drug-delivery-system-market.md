<p><p>The hydrogel-based drug delivery system market is divided into two main types: natural and synthetic systems. Natural hydrogels, derived from biopolymers like alginate or chitosan, are biocompatible and often used for targeted therapies due to their biodegradable properties. In contrast, synthetic hydrogels are engineered through chemical processes, allowing for precise control over their physical and chemical properties. These systems enable tailored drug release profiles, enhancing therapeutic efficacy and improving patient compliance in various medical applications.</p></p>
