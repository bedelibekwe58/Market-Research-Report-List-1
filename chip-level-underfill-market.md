<p><p>The chip level underfill market consists of two primary types: fluid fillers and non-flowing fillers. Fluid fillers are liquid materials that flow and fill the gaps between semiconductor devices and substrates, ensuring enhanced thermal and mechanical stability. In contrast, non-flowing fillers are paste-like substances that do not flow when applied, providing a rigid layer that supports structural integrity. Both types are crucial for improving reliability and performance in electronic packaging, with applications in advanced microelectronics and semiconductor manufacturing.</p></p>
