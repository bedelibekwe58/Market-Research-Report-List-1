<p><p>The open-loop current sensor market is primarily divided into isolated and non-isolated types. Isolated current sensors provide electrical isolation between the sensor output and the circuit being monitored, enhancing safety and protecting sensitive components from high voltages. In contrast, non-isolated sensors directly measure current without this protective barrier, often offering simpler designs and lower costs. Each type serves specific applications based on factors like safety requirements, response times, and circuit configurations, influencing their adoption across various industries.</p></p>
