<p><p>The dairy products culture market comprises three primary types: mesophilic, thermophilic, and probiotics. Mesophilic cultures thrive at moderate temperatures, commonly used in cheeses like cheddar and yogurt, enhancing texture and flavor. Thermophilic cultures operate at higher temperatures, ideal for producing yogurt and certain cheeses like mozzarella, promoting a distinct taste and firmness. Probiotic cultures, rich in beneficial bacteria, are increasingly popular for their health benefits, supporting gut health and overall wellness, and are often found in functional dairy products.</p></p>
