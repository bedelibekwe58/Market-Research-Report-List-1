<p><p>The cardiac monitoring devices market encompasses various methods for tracking heart activity and blood pressure. The ECG method records electrical signals to assess heart rhythm and detect abnormalities. The arterial pressure method involves direct measurement of blood pressure in arteries, providing vital information on cardiac function. The photoelectric method employs light sensors to monitor blood flow and heart rate, often used in pulse oximetry. These diverse approaches cater to different clinical needs, enhancing patient care and outcomes.</p></p>
